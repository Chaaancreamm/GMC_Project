Activated Carbon

Manufacturer: China

Chemical Description

Activated carbon, also known as activated charcoal, is a highly porous form of carbon with a large surface area, making it an excellent adsorbent material. It is produced by heating carbon-rich materials, such as wood, coal, or coconut shells, at high temperatures in the absence of oxygen, which creates a network of tiny pores and increases its surface area.


Activated carbon is widely used across various industries for its exceptional adsorption properties. Its porous structure enables it to effectively capture and remove impurities, contaminants, and pollutants from gases, liquids, and solutions. This makes activated carbon a crucial component in water purification, air and gas filtration, chemical processing, and environmental remediation efforts.


In the mining industry specifically, activated carbon plays a vital role in the extraction of gold and other precious metals from cyanide solutions through a process known as carbon-in-pulp (CIP) or carbon-in-leach (CIL).


In this process, activated carbon is used to adsorb the gold cyanide complex from the solution, allowing for efficient gold recovery. Overall, activated carbon is prized for its versatility, efficiency, and ability to purify a wide range of substances, making it indispensable in various industrial and environmental applications.
